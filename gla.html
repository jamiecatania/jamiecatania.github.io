GovLoop Academy & Learning Platform

Software Engineering & UX Design / Nov 2014 - Oct 2017
 

The Brief

As the saying goes, "Everyone hates their LMS" (Learning Management System). GovLoop needed to deliver and track hundreds of microlearning experiences for their thousands of users. So rather than licensing an LMS we would grow to hate, we built our own. Powered by WordPress and LearnDash to track learning experiences via the Experience API (xAPI), GovLoop Academy was launched in April 2014 with 25 courses available for free to the GovLoop community. Given the success of our newly-minted platform, government clients (who presumably hated their LMSes) started to ask if they could license our platform for their own internal projects. Sensing opportunity, our team jumped at the opportunity to build and implement new features for our clients.

Objective

Design and build a lightweight Learning Management System (LMS) to deliver and track hundreds of microlearning experiences for GovLoop's thousands of users and government clients alike.

My Role

Learning Experience Designer & Software Engineer

My involvement in this project consisted of two major phases, initially as a learning strategist & experience designer and later as a software engineer.

The Story

At the outset of the project, we had to answer some foundational questions:

Who are our users and what do they want?

What are their primary motivations, frustrations, and preferred modes of learning?

What is a 'GovLoop Learning Experience?'

How do we build a uniform, consistent marriage between content & platform experiences?

How do we change users' expectations?

Why is their view of online learning so poor, and what are we going to do differently?

We took a service design approach to answering these questions, including user interviews & focus groups, structured design thinking activities (i.e. "Gamestorming"), and rapid prototyping. While these activities were certainly a team-based effort, my contribution came primarily in the form of strategic planning and facilitation of many of the activities. In addition, I led the effort to distill our observations into actionalable design insights. From those insights, we developed data-driven user personas, user journey maps, and UI wireframes. Perhaps most importantly, we developed a system of design goals and constraints to guide and blend design decisions between both content and platform.

GLA Learning Experience Definition of "Done"

Used to evaluate new content and platform features before release to ensure consistent user experience.

  • Clearly-Defined Learning Objectives

    Clearly articulates what skills or knowledge the learner will gain.

  • Micro-learning

    Experience fits within strict timeline guidelines.

  • Applicable to Real World

    Includes real-world case studies, scenarios, and challenges.

  • Action-oriented

    Includes a call-to-action, action plan, or some other means of transferring learning.

  • Accessible on all devices

    Can be accessed across PCs and mobile devices, including Section 508 compatibility.

  • Content Gateway

    Links to follow-on online and in-person experiences.
