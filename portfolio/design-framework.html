Experience Design Framework

Project Management & Lead Experience Designer / 2014 - 2017

How might we produce a more consistent product quality for our end users and more predictable timelines for our clients?

Objective

Design a framework for rapidly designing and building dozens of consistently high-quality microlearning experiences.

Tabletop simulation playtest session.
Waterfall user research and design process.

My Role

UX Designer & Project Manager

My contributions to the team's efforts primarily came in leading design thinking activities, documenting our insights, and defining our program-wide instructional design system.

Step 1: Instructional Design Plan

Discussion is King. Every interaction in the simulation should contribute to group discussion.

The experience should be dynamic and unpredictable, just like change is in the real world.

The simulation should be designed to be modular, so that the experience can be customized per client.

Result

After eight months of research, design iterations, game development, and user testing, a fully-functioning Minimally Viable Product was completed and delivered to live clients during three daylong pilot sessions. Early feedback indicated a marked improvement in 'flow'-state for users.
