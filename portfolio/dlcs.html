Digital Leadership Challenge Simulation

User Experience Design / 2013 - 2014

A realtime, multiplayer simulation designed to help executives be more effective leaders.

Goal

Digitize and enhance a tabletop leadership-training simulation focused on creating empathy, awareness, and personal accountability for executive leaders of large organizations.

My Role

User Experience (UX) Team Lead

Design Tools:
Balsamiq, Unity Engine, Mind Mapping, Focus Groups, Paper Prototyping

Throughout the life of this project, I led user research and design activities for a 6-person multidisciplinary team consisting of a project manager, two UX designers, a visual designer, and a game developer. This role consisted of coordination with internal stakeholders, building holisitic user research and testing strategies, and owning the design inputs to the final product.

The Story

We started the project with two primary resources: (1) an existing, three-day in-person tabletop leadership simulation and (2) a team of experienced leadership coaches that have facilitated the simulation for several years. We leveraged these resources through playtesting, detailed task analysis, and user observation. As a result, the team grew to understand and appreciate every facet of the tabletop simulation that contributed to a successful user experience.

Our Users

Our primary finding was that only about 23% of users reported a state of 'flow', where the balance of challenge and success created a sense of immersion and growth. Through discussion with stakeholders, we determined that number to be too low, and established the definition of success for our Digital Leadership Challenge Simulation to be closer to 40% of users reporting a state of flow.

Key Design Insights

Discussion is King. Every single interaction should generate group discussion.

The experience should be dynamic and unpredictable, just like change is in the real world.

The simulation should be designed to be modular and customizable per client.

Design Artifacts

Rough sketches of the board overview interface.

Low-fidelity wireframes used to gather user feedback and stakeholder buy-in.

High-fidelity mockups delivered to game developer.

Result

After eight months of research, design iterations, game development, and user testing, a functional minimally-viable product was completed and tested with live clients during three daylong pilot sessions. Early feedback indicated a marked improvement in 'flow'-state for users.
