Human Trafficking Awareness Compaign

Learning Experience Design / 2015 - 2016

How might we encourage members of the public to take an active role in combating human trafficking in the United States?

Objective

Design and deliver a series of online courses for government employees and the general public to learn how to recognize and report the red flag indicators of human trafficking activity around them.

My Role

Lead Learning Experience Designer

I was responsible for all learning/content strategy, scriptwriting & storyboarding, and interactive web development throughout the curriculum. I also collaborated with video editors to produce all video content used in the campaign.

Design Tools Used:
Storyline 2, Illustrator, InDesign, Premiere, Javascript

The Story

To help put an end to human trafficking in the United States and around the world, Congress has tasked federal agencies, like the Department of Homeland Security (DHS), to work in partnership with the private-sector to end human trafficking. As a part of this effort, our team collaborated with DHS' Blue Campaign to build a highly-interactive digital awareness and education campaign for DHS employees and members of the general public.

Detailed interaction flow chart.

We conducted our initial research phase through three primary means: (1) interviewing Subject Matter Experts from across law enforcement and administration, (2) reviewing existing Blue Campaign awareness materials, and most importantly, (3) a series of intense, emotional interviews with survivors of human trafficking. The major design insight we took away from our research was that in order to convince people to take action, we would need to instill an emotional connection between our users and the victims of human trafficking.
